About Me

Senior at University of Wisconsin - Madison majoring in electrical engineering and computer science. Courses include Databases (systems level), Mobile Apps (Android), Machine Learning (supervised), Networking, Software Engineering, Algorithms, Numerical Methods, Digital Signal Processing and Microprocessors.

I had three internships during my college career:

The first I worked part-time (20hr/week) during my Sophomore year (Aug 2015 – May 2015) for Maverick Software Consulting, which then contracted me to Thompson Reuters, a legal software company. I worked on a rigorous data scrapping projects to automate reading of legal documents and extracting metadata.

The second was at Philips Radiation Oncology Systems from May 2015 – May 2016. I did a wide variety of things for this job, including networking work with DICOM, lots of linux DevOps , and IMPT work for optimizing Bragg interpolation.

The third was at Cray, Inc, the supercomputing company, from May 2016 – Aug 2016. I worked on a better version of valgrind, optimized for a distributed system (MPI libraries). During the internship I learned a lot about optimizing code for different heterogeneous environments and workload managers. I also had the chance to learn how to do hybrid programming.
